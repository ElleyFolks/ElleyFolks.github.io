About Me

I’ve completed my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Western Governors University and am nearing completion of my Master’s in Data Analytics with a specialization in Data Science, also from WGU. This academic journey builds on a foundation that includes chemistry, physics, and engineering from my Associate of Science in Engineering. I’m a CompTIA Project+ certified professional with hands-on experience in software development, machine learning, and full-stack applications.

Over the past year, I’ve deepened my focus on machine learning, training models using TensorFlow and PyTorch to solve real-world problems. In my current internship, I’ve especially enjoyed collecting, cleaning, and analyzing data—developing both technical workflows and an appreciation for how raw data becomes meaningful insight. I’ve recently been exploring digital signal processing and its intersection with machine learning, which has expanded my interest in real-time systems and low-level computation.

I’m proficient in a range of programming languages including Python, Java, C++, HTML, and CSS, and have also worked with the Unity engine to develop various game projects. I thrive on learning new tools and techniques, constantly evolving to keep pace with a fast-moving tech landscape.

I am curious, tenacious, and enthusiastic—a pursuer of excellence and knowledge. I hold myself to high standards and take pride in a disciplined, smart work ethic grounded in prioritization and adaptability. I see every challenge as an opportunity for growth. Whether I succeed or fail, I learn—and I iterate.

When working in teams, I draw from the skills, knowledge, and perspectives of others. I value clear communication and am not afraid to ask questions, seek feedback, or offer support. Collaboration, to me, means leveraging the best of what each person brings to the table.

I found my passion for computer science during the final semester of my engineering degree, in an introductory C++ course. It clicked—this was the path I had been looking for. Programming gave me a new way to apply mathematics, creativity, and logic. As I advanced, I also discovered a deep interest in data analytics and data science—fields that combine statistical reasoning with real-world impact. Since then, I’ve pursued computer science and data-driven problem solving with direction and purpose.

Outside of work and study, I love the outdoors. You can often find me hiking, camping, bouldering, or digging for crystals. I’m an avid reader (Wheel of Time and Dune are among my favorites) and enjoy gaming with friends during my downtime.
